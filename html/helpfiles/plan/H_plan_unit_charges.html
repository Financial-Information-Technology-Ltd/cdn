Initial Charges

Initial charges (sometimes known as entrance fees) are taken automatically by deduction from premium on payment.

They post a credit to accounting pseudo ENTR which should be mapped to the corporate chart of accounts ledger number at Product Workbench > Accounting.

The charge is designed to cover the up front costs of administration, including commission expenses.

Options are:

1 - Specific value

2 - Percentage of API (annual premium income)/SPI (single premium income)

3 - Number of net mode premiums

4 - No. of net mode prems minus pol fee

5 - Percentage of (API/SPI MINUS policy fee)

6 - Percentage of Gross Modal Premium

7 - Maximum - %ge of Gross Modal Premium

8 - Percentage of initial premium

9 - Percentage of lowest premium within clawback period, plus premium reduction uplift (if applicable)

Establishment Charges

Establishment charges are triggered by a EST future record, processed by batch job LFSEST. The charge is paid by cancelling units to its value.

The charge is designed to cover the up front costs of administration, including commission expenses.

         s Semi-annually

Incidence

In combination with frequency above, this will determine the date of the first EST future record. If in advance, it will be on policy issue date. If in arrears, it will be one frequency after issue.

Reduction Fee

Reduction fees are triggered by a RDN future record, created following a premium reduction endorsement, and processed by batch job LFSRDN. The fee is paid by cancelling units to its value and is sometimes called a maintenance charge.

They are designed to compensate the insurance company for the fact that lower income than expected will be received (though the allocation rate or premium-related fee), as well as the administration cost of processing the premium reduction.

Basis

Select the reduction fee calculation basis from the dropdown list.

Options are:

         1 - Fixed amount

         2 - Factor X + ((100-Initial Period in months)/12) - Commission Clawback Adjustment

Amount

Enter the fixed amount of the reduction fee if appropriate (calculation option 1 above)

Frequency

Select the frequency of the reduction charge from the dropdown list.

Options are:

         M monthly

         X one-off

No reduction on Status Change to Paid-up

Tick if reduction fee does not apply for policies changing to a paid-up status. By default, changing to paid-up does count as a premium reduction endorsement and a reduction fee applies.

** Initial Units, Company Units and Company Unit Accounting (Actuarial Funding)

In the UK there is a method of accounting that is often used, called company unit accounting or actuarial funding. In this method, policies allocate their premium to initial units at the beginning the term, and to accumulation units thereafter. The initial unit term is a product parameter.

Initial units are subject to a percentage management charge. Accumulation units are not subject to this charge.

When initial units are used, insurance companies like to account for their charge "up front" for the expected duration of the policy. The way this is done is by actuarial funding - which involves the creation of company units. Essentially, whenever a policy unit movement occurs, there is a matching unit movement in "company units".

The company unit movement is in proportion to the policy unit movement. The proportion is stored on a table and depends on the expired term of the policy in months. Typically, this proportion increases to 100% over the initial allocation period of the policy (and stays at 100% thereafter). Unit accounting is created based on the number of company unit moves, and there is an extra posting for the difference between the policy unit movement value and the company unit movement value.

The only policy unit movement that does not generate a matching company unit movement is the management charge on initial units (described above). In this way, the number of policy units gradually reduces over the life of the policy and ends up the same as the number of company units. Or at least, that's the actuarial theory.

Policies that are surrendered early tend to be subject to a penalty against the initial units to allow for the charge (which was accounted for "up front") that will not now be received.
