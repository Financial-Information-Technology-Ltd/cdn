Plan Actuarial Pre-funding details

In the UK there is a method of accounting that is often used, called company unit accounting or actuarial pre-funding. In this method, policies allocate their premium to initial units at the beginning the term, and to accumulation units thereafter. The initial unit term is a product parameter.

Initial units are subject to a percentage management charge. Accumulation units are not subject to this charge. The charge is triggered by an IUC future record, processed by batch job LFSUAL3.

When initial units are used, insurance companies like to account for their charge "up front" (i.e. the charge is pre-funded) for the expected duration of the policy. The way this is done is by actuarial funding - which involves the creation of company units. Essentially, whenever a policy unit movement occurs, there is a matching unit movement in "company units".

The company unit movement is in proportion to the policy unit movement. The proportion is stored on a table, defined below, and depends on the expired term of the policy in months. Typically, this proportion increases to 100% over the initial allocation period of the policy (and stays at 100% thereafter). Unit accounting is created based on the number of company unit moves, and there is an extra posting for the difference between the policy unit movement value and the company unit movement value.

The only policy unit movement that does not generate a matching company unit movement is the management charge on initial units (described above). In this way, the number of policy units gradually reduces over the term defined below and ends up the same as the number of company units. Or at least, that's the actuarial theory.

Policies that are surrendered early tend to be subject to a penalty against the initial units to allow for the charge (which was accounted for "up front") that will not now be received.
