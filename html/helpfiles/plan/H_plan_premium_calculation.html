Age Adjustment Details

This feature is used where a single premium or mortality rate table is used for joint life policies. The rate age to use is determined by adding a number to the younger lifes age, based on the difference between the ages of the lives assured. It stems from old product designs which were constrained by the amount of data that computers could store. In modern computer systems, there is no problem in holding separate rates for each life.

Age Spread

Number of years between age of each life assured

Adjustment

Number of years to add to the younger lifes age to get the rate age to use in reading the table

Female Setback

For females, the number of years to deduct from the age in order to read the mortality or premium rate table.

Again this stems from old product designs which were constrained by the amount of data that computers could store; a single table was used for both male and female rates. In modern computer systems, there is no problem in holding separate rates for males and females.

Joint Life Female Setback

For females in joint life policies, the number of years to deduct from the age in order to read the mortality or premium table. This is distinct from female setback above, for product where the setback only applies for joint life policies (i.e. for single life policies, females are charged the same as males).

Once again this stems from old product designs which were constrained by the amount of data that computers could store; a single table was used for both male and female rates. In modern computer systems, there is no problem in holding separate rates for males and females, and different rates for single or joint life policies.

Smoker Setahead

For smokers, the number of years to add to their actual age in order to read the mortality or premium rate table.

Yet again this stems from old product designs which were constrained by the amount of data that computers could store; a single table was used for both smoker and non-smoker rates. In modern computer systems, there is no problem in holding separate rates for smokers and non-smokers.

Non-Smoker Discount rate

For smokers, a discount factor to be applied to the rate read from the mortality or premium rate table.

Once again this stems from old product designs, as explained above.

Minimum Female Setback Age

For product where female setback is used, as described above, the minimum age to be used.

e.g. if a mortality table starts at age 18, this will be set to 18. If is a female setback of 3 years, females who are aged 18, 19, 20 or 21 will use the rate for age 18. Females aged 22 will use the rate for age 19.
