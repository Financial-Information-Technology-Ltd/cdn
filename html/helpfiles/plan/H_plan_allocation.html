Plan Allocation

Settings for allocation rates for unit-linked savings policies, determining the percentage of premium paid that is invested in units.

Parameters here give great flexibility, for example offering higher rates for bigger premiums and, by use of rate tables, it is possible to vary allocation by factors such as policy term, expired duration and policyholder age.

For regular premium policies, companies often give low allocation rates at the outset of a policy, to compensate them for up front expenses such as commission and administrative overheads. Higher allocation rates are given later in the policys life to encourage retention (i.e. to reward policyholders who keep paying premiums).

Allocation Adjustment Details
