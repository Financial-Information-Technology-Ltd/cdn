Plan Premium Billing

Parameters determining the generation of bills (renewal notices). Bills are triggered by a BI future record, processed by batch job LF.GENERATE.RENEWAL.NOTICE.

In addition, premium notices can be issued based on settings on this screen. Premium notices can be issued for regular premiums, in addition to the annual renewal notice. They are triggered by a PN future record which is processed by batch job LF.GENERATE.PAYMENT.NOTICE.

Pay premium even if received before due date

Tick if premium should be paid when received before due date.

If unticked, and premium is received before it is due, it will be credited to policy suspense. This option can be used for risk products to avoid overstating premium income.

Billing Frequencies

Select renewal notice frequency from the dropdown list:

Options are:

         A - Annually

         S - Semi-Annually

         Q - Quarterly

         M Monthly

Billing day of the month

Select the day of the month that renewal notices should be issued from the dropdown list.

Create bill at complete pending status

Tick if the first bill should be created automatically when a policy is "complete" at new business entry (i.e. no underwriting is outstanding). The complete pending status is set at Admin > Configuration > Statuses, "Ready to Issue".

Billing rules

Select billing rules from the dropdown list.

Options are:

         0 - Bill always

         1 - Do not bill when premium is not due

         2 - Do not bill when previous bill is not paid

Soft billing

Tick if "soft billing" is to be used. In soft billing, bills are issued for the expected premium, but any money received is paid as premium. It is typically used for unit-linked or deposit administration savings policies.

The opposite is "hard billing", where premium must be paid as billed (expected). This is always used for protection policies. If insufficient premium is paid, it remains in policy suspense.
