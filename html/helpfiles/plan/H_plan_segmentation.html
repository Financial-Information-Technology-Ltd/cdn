Plan Segmentation

Segmentation is the administration of multiple policies under a single wrapper policy. It is used for different reasons in different markets.

UK

A feature which is common in single premium investment bond products

All segments must all have same start date, end date and lives assured

Also known as clustering

Done for tax reasons - full surrenders are treated differently from partial surrenders. It looks (to the tax man) as if you are fully surrendering a little policy, not partially surrendering a big one

The number of Segments" is entered at new business

When a surrender is required, the segments are sliced off at Policy Workbench > Partial Surrender. Withdrawal Code Segment Surrender

If unit prices exist for the processing date, the "slicing" is performed online.

If not, a PSUR future record is created. This is processed by batch job LFPTSR.

In the "slicing"

a separate policy record is created for each segment being surrendered.

its number is the base policy number with a "/nn" suffix (the numbers count down from the highest value)

the new policies are immediately surrendered

Unit movement on the "original" policy (units deducted) but no unit accounting

Unit reason is USL

Policy accounting for the surrender on the new policies

Luxembourg

Segmentation is done for reasons of investment efficiency to achieve the minimum investment fund size required for a tax efficient investment

Segments can have

different owner/owners (typically related family members)

different life/lives assured

different levels of life cover

different beneficiaries

different tax statuses

The split of total premium across segments is entered at new business

Each segment is created as a new policy record at issue

Other

Luxembourg style segmentation has also been used for education protection products, as it allows different beneficiaries (the children) on separate policies administered as one for premium processing, owned by the parent.
