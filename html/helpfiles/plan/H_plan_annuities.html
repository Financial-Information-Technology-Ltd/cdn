Plan Annuities

Parameters for annuity products for policies which pay a regular income after receipt of a lump sum premium.

The details of each annuity are stored on a record on the ANNUITY_PAYMENT table, not on the policy record itself. There is a hyperlnk at the bottom of the policy workbench to see details of its annuities.

Annuity payment is triggered by an ANPP future record, processed by batch job LFBANP Annuity Payments.

Label

Description

Annuity type

Select the annuity type from the dropdown list.

Options are:

         J Job Loss

         R Retirement

This parameter allows the batch job for processing annuity payments (LFBANP Anuity Payments) to be run separately for one specific annuity type. The setting here (J or R) can be used as the batch parameter - using J will ensure only job loss annuities are processed, and R will ensure only retirement annuities are processed.

This can be used, for example, if retirement annuity payments are processed once a month but job loss annuiities are processed daily as they fall due.

Annuity calculate code

Select the annuity calculation code from the dropdown list.

Options are:

         1 - Sum Invested * Rate (percent)

         3 - Fixed Amount

         4 - Rate * Sum Invested / Annuity Term

         5 - Sum Assured * Rate (percent)

         6 - Annuity Calculation from first principles

Rate Table to calculate annuity

Where a rate table is used, select the table from the dropdown list. The dropdown list is comprised of tables defined at Support > Rates > Scheme Rates

For calculation option 6 above, this is the mortality table used in the calculation.

Rate value to calculate annuity (%)

Where the calculation uses a fixed percentage rate (calculation options 1 and 5 above), enter the value here.

Annuity amount (annual)

Where the annuity is a fixed annual amount (calculation option 3 above), enter the amount here.

Gross Yield Rate

Used in conjunction with calculation option 6 - Annuity Calculation from first principles.

Select the rate table from the dropdown list. The dropdown list is comprised of tables defined at Support > Rates > Scheme Rates.

This rate represents the investment yield used in the calculation of the annuity.

Per Policy Initial Expense

Used in conjunction with calculation option 6 - Annuity Calculation from first principles.

Select the rate table from the dropdown list. The dropdown list is comprised of tables defined at Support > Rates > Scheme Rates.

This rate represents the initial expense amount used in the calculation of the annuity.

Per Policy Renewal Fixed Expense

Used in conjunction with calculation option 6 - Annuity Calculation from first principles.

Select the rate table from the dropdown list. The dropdown list is comprised of tables defined at Support > Rates > Scheme Rates.

This rate represents the fixed renewal expense amount used in the calculation of the annuity.

Variable Expense (% of annuity)

Used in conjunction with calculation option 6 - Annuity Calculation from first principles.

Select the rate table from the dropdown list. The dropdown list is comprised of tables defined at Support > Rates > Scheme Rates.

This rate represents the variable expense percentage used in the calculation of the annuity.

Writing Agent Commission Rate

Used in conjunction with calculation option 6 - Annuity Calculation from first principles.

Select the rate table from the dropdown list. The dropdown list is comprised of tables defined at Support > Rates > Scheme Rates.

This rate represents the commission percentage used in the calculation of the annuity. It is a total commission percentage, allowing for standard overrides as well as writing agent commission itself.

Expense Inflation rate

Used in conjunction with calculation option 6 - Annuity Calculation from first principles.

Select the rate table from the dropdown list. The dropdown list is comprised of tables defined at Support > Rates > Scheme Rates.

This rate represents the expense inflation rate used in the calculation of the annuity.

Contingency and Profit Margin

Used in conjunction with calculation option 6 - Annuity Calculation from first principles.

Select the rate table from the dropdown list. The dropdown list is comprised of tables defined at Support > Rates > Scheme Rates.

This rate represents the amount allowed for contingency and profit margin in the calculation of the annuity.

Allowed escalation rates

Enter the permitted annual escalation rates for annuities in payment.

The user will select from this list at new business entry.

Allowed reversionary percentages

Enter the permitted reversionary (spouse pension) percentages for annuities.

For joint life annuities (i.e. where a spouses details are entered at new business), the user will select from this list at new business entry. Selecting this option reduces the initial annuity amount.

On death of the first annuitant, this proportion of the original annuity will be paid to the surviving spouse until death.

Allowed guaranteed periods (months)

Enter the possible guaranteed periods for annuities.

The user will select from this list at new business entry.

On death of the annuitant within the guaranteed period, the annuity will still be paid until the end of the guaranteed period. Selecting this option reduces the annuity amount.

School Term Dates

For annuity products designed to pay school fees on a termly basis, enter the school term dates on which the annuity will be paid.

e.g. for term dates 1 January, 1 April and 1 September, enter

1 1

1 4

1 9

The next ANPP (payment trigger) future records will be set as each payment is made. E.g. When the 1 September payment is made, the next ANPP will be created for the following 1 January.

Annuity term (months)

For annuities with a fixed term, enter the term in months here.

This is commonly used for job loss annuities where the benefit is paid for a maximum of n months, or until the insured restarts work, if earlier.

Annuity term table (months)

For annuities with a rate table dependent term, select the table from the dropdown list.

The dropdown list is comprised of tables defined at Support > Rates > Scheme Rates.

Annuity payment frequencies and methods

Enter the permitted combinations of annuity payment frequency and payment method.

The user will select from this list at new business entry.

Activate annuity when policy matures

Tick this box if the annuity is activated on policy maturity.

This is usually used where the annuity is a rider benefit on a savings product.

Mature/Expire coverage or policy when all annuity payments are finished

Set this parameter so that the policy will expire when the last annuity payment has been made.

Apply loading when calculating annuity payment

Tick this option if mortality loading is to be used in calculating the annuity amount.

The loading is a percentage, and can be either positive (to reduce the annuity amount) or negative (to increase the annuity amount).

When annuity starts

Defines when the first annuity payment (Future record ANPP) will be generated based on the Effective Date of the Policy. If the Effective Date is not set at New Business entry, it will be set to the System date when the policy is Activated.

Options are:

         E - Effective Date

         M - Month after Effective Date

         N - Next Modal Date after Effective Date

Cash Back (percent of original payment)

Enter the cashback percentage, if any, to be paid on annuity issue.

A payment out will be created for a policy on its status change to active for this percentage of the premium.

Suspend Annuity Payment Date (DDMM)

For companies who perform an annual check of annuitants to see that they are still alive, enter the date from which payments are suspended each year unless confirmation of existence has been received.

Batch job LF.SUSPEND.ANNUITY.OUTSIDE.GUARANTEE.PERIOD is run in advance of this date, to set a suspension date on each annuity and send a letter/email to each annuitant requesting that they contact the company to confirm they are still alive.

On contacting the company, the suspend payment flag on the annuity is unticked, and payments continue. If confirmation is received after payments have been suspended, any missed payments will be paid.
