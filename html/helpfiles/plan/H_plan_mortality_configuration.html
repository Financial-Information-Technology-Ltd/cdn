Plan Mortality Configuration

Parameters for the timing and frequency of unit-linked or deposit administration mortality premium deduction.

Used for unit-linked or deposit administration savings products with integrated life insurance cover. A UMD future record is created on policy issue according to the settings here. The UMD future record is processed by batch job LFSUAL4, and the mortality premium is calculated according to parameters on screen Product Workbench > Mortality Calculation and paid by cancelling units in proportion to units held on the policy, or by reducing the deposit administration value. LFSUAL4 then creates the next UMD future record for one frequency ahead.

* Initial Units, Company Units and Company Unit Accounting (Actuarial Funding)

In the UK there is a method of accounting that is often used, called company unit accounting or actuarial funding. In this method, policies allocate their premium to initial units at the beginning the term, and to accumulation units thereafter. The initial unit term is a product parameter.

Initial units are subject to a percentage management charge. Accumulation units are not subject to this charge.

When initial units are used, insurance companies like to account for their charge "up front" for the expected duration of the policy. The way this is done is by actuarial funding - which involves the creation of company units. Essentially, whenever a policy unit movement occurs, there is a matching unit movement in "company units".

The company unit movement is in proportion to the policy unit movement. The proportion is stored on a table and depends on the expired term of the policy in months. Typically, this proportion increases to 100% over the initial allocation period of the policy (and stays at 100% thereafter). Unit accounting is created based on the number of company unit moves, and there is an extra posting for the difference between the policy unit movement value and the company unit movement value.

The only policy unit movement that does not generate a matching company unit movement is the management charge on initial units (described above). In this way, the number of policy units gradually reduces over the life of the policy and ends up the same as the number of company units. Or at least, that's the actuarial theory.

Policies that are surrendered early tend to be subject to a penalty against the initial units to allow for the charge (which was accounted for "up front") that will not now be received.
