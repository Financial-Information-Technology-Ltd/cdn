Underwriting Table

An underwriting table is a matrix with applicant age and sum assured level as its axes. It defines which new business underwriting requirements apply depending on how old the applicant is, and how much life cover he/she is applying for.

The underwriting table applicable for a product is defined at Support > Product Workbench, Underwriting, Underwriting table. LIFEfit automatically checks the table on creation of a new policy, and creates the underwriting checks as necessary.

Table Details

On creating a new table, you are prompted for the Sum Assured limits and Age limits that form the columns and rows of the table.

n.b. for the first column, any requirements you specify will apply for sums at risk up to this amount. So if there is a level at which no underwriting is required, you will need a blank cell. For the second and subsequent columns, you define the upper boundary requirements apply from the sum at risk in the previous column, up to the sum at risk in this column. The same applies for the rows - you define the upper age band at which requirements apply.

For an existing table, the rows and columns are fixed and display only. On clicking a cell in the table, a pop-up window appears, listing all the possible underwriting requirements (as defined at Support > Underwriting > Requirements). You tick the ones that apply at the age/sum at risk combination you have selected.
