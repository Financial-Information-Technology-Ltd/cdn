Unit Linked Fund Codes Detail

Details of a unit-linked investment fund.

A unit-linked fund is a pool of assets that many policyholders invest in. It is looked after by a fund manager. The fund is divided into units, each unit representing an equal share of the fund. The price of the units varies over time and depends on the value of the assets in the fund. The unit price can go down as well as up and is not guaranteed.

Companies typically offer many different funds in which policyholders may invest. Each fund has investment objectives, which set out the type and mix of assets that the fund invests in.

Dealing Frequency

Enter the frequency of fund pricing.

Future records for unit-related actions (investment, charging, surrender, maturity, death claim etc.) are created on the next day for which prices for all funds involved in the transaction are expected.

This ensures transactions are only attempted when prices should be available, rather than trying every day in batch until they appear.

Dealing Day

For weekly-priced funds, the day of the week on which prices are published.

Day of month

For monthly-priced funds, the day of the month on which prices are published.

Cut-off days

Where funds are priced monthly, the day of the month after which the next unit processing future record triggers should be created for the following month.

e.g. if the fund is priced on the 9 th of the month, and you enter 2 here, any future records created on or after the 3 rd of January will be for the 9 th of February. Future records created on the 1 st and 2 nd of January will be for the 9 th of January.

Performance Fee (%)

Enter the performance fee percentage.

Performance Fee processing is performed at the start of the calendar year. It calculates fees for each policy, based on units purchased during the previous calendar year (one fee amount per unit transaction). Units are cancelled to the value of the fees.

e.g. if three unit purchases have been made for a policy in the year, three separate fee amounts are calculated and charged by cancelling units.

It is processed by batch job LF.PERFORMANCE.FEE which produces report PERFEE.CSV.

For switch outs and surrenders (partial / full), the fee is calculated at the time of the processing and excluded from the calculation at the end of the year.

Performance Fee: AM share (%)

Enter the asset managers percentage share of the performance fee.

Trailer commission rate (%)

Enter the trailer commission percentage, if fund-specific.

Trailer commission is paid as a percentage of fund value on active and paid-up policies, according to the frequency defined on the product workbench. It is triggered by a UTC future record.

A default percentage is defined at product level, but can be overridden here at fund level.

Distribution section

Distribution involves a regular declaration of a dividend on a fund, typically quarterly or semi-annually. The dividend is declared as a currency amount per unit e.g. 6.5 pence per unit. A batch process, initiated online, processes and accounts for the value of the dividend according to the rules defined here.

For more information, see screens at Administration > Fund Distribution.

Month / Action

Select the month and associated distribution action from the drop down lists.

Distribution actions are:

        1 Distribute (i.e. process "pence per unit" according to policy rules, which may be to reinvest, pay income, or pay a fixed income amount and reinvest or cancel units as necessary to make the balance)

        2 Value and Advise Next Distribution Date (some funds distribute semi-annually, and advise on the other quarter-years)

        3 Valuation only (i.e. no actual distribution processing required)

More about Mirror Unit Functionality - Terminal Bonus & Market Value Adjustment

Concerns asset values in unitised with profit funds - and how to calculate the terminal bonus / market value adjustment (TB/MVA) at surrender, partial surrender or maturity

At surrender or maturity, companies may compare the policy value and the mirror value. If the difference is less than x% (typically 5%), the policy value is paid. If more than x%, the mirror value is paid.

Bonus Guaranteed funds

        Every movement in a "visible" fund is reflected by a movement in its mirror fund. The only exception to this is movements for surrenders and partial surrenders, where there is an adjustment to the amount paid out to reflect the difference between "visible" and "mirror" policy values.

        Number of units moved in mirror fund is (value of units moved in "visible" fund / mirror fund unit price)

With Profits second series

        Same as for bonus guaranteed PLUS

        Every time the bid price of the visible fund increases, 1/9 of the increase in value is deducted from the mirror fund. This is the shareholders' cut.

With Profits series 1

        Same as for With Profits second series PLUS

        There are 2 mirror funds - E (equity and property) and F (fixed interest)

        At the outset, the policy is 100% E. Two thirds of the way into its term, the policy starts to be switched so that, at maturity, the split is 100-x% E x%F (x is typically 20%). This switch is done monthly over the last third of the policy term. In addition, there is daily rebalancing of the mirror funds to maintain the correct split (which would otherwise be altered due to changes in the relative prices of the E and F mirror funds)

This functionality only impacts at policy level when working out the TB/MVA.
